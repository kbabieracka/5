Guinea Pig Nutrition

 

 

 

 

  ►Adult Guinea Pig nutrition requirements (over 6 months):

  • 1 cup of fresh vegetables per guinea pig per day

  • Small amounts of fruit usually given a couple times per week

  • Unlimited grass hay (Timothy, Orchard Grass, Bluegrass, Bermuda Grass, etc.)

  • Unlimited water

  • A good quality plain pellet with no added seeds/nuts or other bits. Adults-1/8 to 1/4 cup per day.  A good pellet for adult guinea pigs is Oxbow's Cavy Cuisine.

  • Forages

  ►Young Guinea Pig nutrition requirements (under 6 months):

  • 1 cup of fresh vegetables per guinea pig per day

  • Small amounts of fruit usually given a couple times per week

  • Unlimited Alfalfa hay and Unlimited grass hay

  • Unlimited water

  • A good quality plain pellet with no added seeds/nuts or other bits..  Feed unlimited amounts.     A good pellet for young guinea pigs is Oxbow's Cavy Performance.

  • Forages

  ►The keys to a good diet for your guinea pigs are to:

  • Use a wide variety of vegetables and fruits.

  • Feed 2 servings of leafy greens daily.

  • Feed 1 serving of veggies daily.

  • Feed 1/2 serving of fruits.

  • Feed forages daily when available.  *See bottom of page for more Forage information.

  • Feed unlimited hay.  Hay helps assist with digestion and is essential to wearing down molars.

  • Feed a good quality plain pellet with stabilized Vitamin C.

  • Use vegetables/fruits low in calcium (calcium can cause bladder sludge/stones).

  • Use vegetables/fruits low in oxalic acid (can bind with calcium and form oxalate stones).

  • Aim for a good balance between Calcium:Phosphorus (Ca:Ph) keeping the overall ratio between 1.5:1 and 2:1.  There is a great Ca:Ph ratio calculator on the nutrition page at    Guinea Lynx.

  • Avoid feeding large portions of vegetables/fruits high in sugars and starches.

  • Avoid pet store "treats" such as yoghurt drops, etc.

  • Avoid breads and dairy products as cavies are strict herbivores.

  • Supplement cavies with Vitamin C if they will not eat vegetables/fruits or if they are ill.  Use a plain Vitamin C tablet with no sugar added.  Give approximately 25-50mg per day.  You also may use a liquid Vitamin C that you can dose using a syringe.  Do NOT use Vitamin C drops that you put in water.  The drops degrade quickly in water and may distort the taste of the water making your pig drink less.  Do not use Tang in your guinea pigs water.

  Forages, if and when available, are great to include in your guinea pigs diet.  All forages must be

  free of chemicals/pesticides and free from other animal feces/urine.  Beware of any plants that may  

  be poisonous.  If you are unsure if it is safe, dont feed it.  Click on photo to see larger image.
